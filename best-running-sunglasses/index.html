Sunglasses UV Index & Polarized Lens Advisor Guide

Deciding which sunglasses to wear depends on your local UV index rating and atmospheric conditions. Ultraviolet (UV) radiation ranges from low (0-2) to extreme (11+). While cloud cover diffuses visible light, up to 80% of UV rays still penetrate, meaning daily eye protection is critical.

ShadeCast maps local coordinate telemetry dynamically to recommend correct lens categories. Category 0-1 tints (clear, yellow, rose) are optimized for night or overcast flatness, while Category 3-4 (charcoal, dark brown, mirrored finishes) protect eyes from harsh glacial snow reflection or high-altitude sun.
